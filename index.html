In gardens where the wild winds play, And crimson blooms steal light from day, There grows a rose of softer hue— Not bold of red, nor pale of blue. It blushes like a whispered name, A secret love without the flame. Its petals speak in tender tones, Of hearts that bloom in quiet zones. No thorns declare a fight or pride, It simply stands, with grace inside. A rose for love not rushed or loud— But faithful, warm, and gently proud. It’s not the spark that scorches fast, But warmth in love that longs to last. So if I gift this bloom to you, Know every petal says: “I do.” .

Oh, and finally ...

I dance where colors dare to flame, My every feather speaks my name. The earth adores my royal plume, A bloom of fire, a living bloom. My love is fierce, a song, a spark— A beating heart within the dark.

And once—by chance, or fate, or star— Our paths aligned from worlds afar. Where mirror-lake meets meadow's gold, Where heat and hush both dare be bold. No voice declared what we should be, Yet all of nature bent to see.

For love is not a mirrored face, But contrast held in kind embrace. Not matching hues or mirrored forms, But shelter shared through life’s long storms. You bring the blaze, I bring the breeze— We make our peace where both find ease.
